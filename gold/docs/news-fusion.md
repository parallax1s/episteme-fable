# Private Fusion Firm Claims Sustained Net-Energy Shot

Helion-rival Pacific Fusion announced Thursday that its prototype reactor sustained a net-energy-producing plasma for 41 seconds, which would be the longest confinement of its kind if confirmed. The company said the shot produced 1.9 times the energy injected into the fuel, though it acknowledged that the figure excludes the power consumed by the reactor's magnets and cooling systems.

Independent researchers urged caution. Plasma physicist Dr. Ingrid Halvorsen of the Max Planck Institute said the claimed gain "would be a genuine milestone if the diagnostics hold up," but noted that the company has not yet released calorimetry data or submitted the results for peer review. Previous private-sector fusion claims have shrunk substantially under scrutiny; a 2024 announcement by another startup was later revised downward by a factor of three.

Pacific Fusion's chief executive said the company plans to begin construction of a pilot power plant in 2027 and to deliver electricity to the grid by 2032. Most academic roadmaps place grid-connected fusion no earlier than the 2040s. If the company's timeline holds, it would compress the standard projection by more than a decade — a gap that skeptics say reflects marketing pressure from an oversubscribed funding round rather than any physics breakthrough.
